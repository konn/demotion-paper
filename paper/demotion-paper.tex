\begin{abstract}
  We propose to think \emph{constructively} in designing embedded type-systems in moderately dependently typed language, such as Haskell.
  Our beloved Curry--Howard correspondence tells us that types are propositions and programs are proofs in intuitionistic logic, in a rigorous sense.
  According to Brouwer--Heyting--Kolmogorov interpretation, its informal ancestor, a proof of an intuitionistic proposition is some kind of a construction, or \emph{witness}, conveying the information of the proposition.
  We adopt this rather loose point of view as the guiding principle for developing type-level hacks: give a compiler constructive witnesses when one writes type-level functions or constraints.
  This allows us to design embedded type-systems that play in harmony with their expression-level counterparts.
\end{abstract}
